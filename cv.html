As an ambitious and conscientious year 12 student, starting A-level courses this year (Maths, Further Maths, Physics, Electronics), I am focussed on engineering as my career goal. Over the next 2 years at King Edward VI Grammar School, I hope to further explore and engage in different engineering disciplines, namely the broad branches of mechanical, electronic, and computer (hardware & software) engineering. I learn about engineering through my independent research, projects I undertake, courses I take (e.g., Harvard's CS50 course), and participation in work experience programmes, and various other events like Edventurer's AI and Innovation workshop. My experience in engineering stems from the school Young Engineers Club, which I have participated in since year 8 (except year 9 during COVID restrictions) and where I mentor lower school participants. The year 10 team project I led received a Gold CREST award and a ‘Highly Commended’ award from the Chelmsford Science and Engineering Society. The year 11 team project for the PA Pi competition to 'accelerate the energy transition' reached the national finals, received a Silver CREST award, and a 'Technical Design (Y10-11)' from CSES. To widen my horizons and try new things, I am currently undertaking the Gold DofE award, and have achieved Bronze. I have participated in Felsted's International Summer School which has enhanced my cultural awareness and taught me about relevant global issues through the Global Studies lessons.
