Projects

The projects I embark on are team projects as part of my school's Young Engineers Club.

Year 10: Care-home project (responsible for TypeScript code, case design using TinkerCAD, and research) focussed on detection and prevention of falls, whilst preserving residents’ dignity using a BBC micro:bit and its TypeScript editor. The device would inform staff of detected falls and display other important real-time data via. the serial port on a computer. The code extensively analysed accelerometer and temperature data, transmitted it, and provided code for extensive analysis of a pulse oximeter (which would help forecast falls according to research), although integration was not achieved due to hardware incompatibility. The project achieved the following:

  • ‘Highly Commended’ award (2022) – Chelmsford Science and Engineering Society TEXPO competition
  • Gold CREST award (2022)

Year 11: Project for the PA Raspberry Pi Competition, a solution to energy wastage through household lighting. It is able to detect the artificial light coming from your household lighting and provide easy-to-read data to show when you have been wasting energy (either lights on with no-one in the room or when it’s daytime outside). It can be accessed from a web app on any browser, including mobile, which will provide up to date statistics on your total spendings and allow for you to track up to date spendings on household lighting with the help of a Raspberry Pi. The essential information such as times when energy has been used on lighting, monthly spendings on energy and equivalent carbon dioxide emissions can be seen from the homepage. Data from individual days can be looked up and trends over a longer period of time can be tracked, allowing you to make better choices about your light usage. This project achieved the following:

  • ‘Technical Design Award (Year 10-11)’ award (2023) – Chelmsford Science and Engineering Society TEXPO competition
  • Silver CREST award (2023)
